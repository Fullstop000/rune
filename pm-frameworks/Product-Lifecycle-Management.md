# Product Lifecycle Management (产品生命周期管理)

**Category:** Systems Thinking (系统思维)

**Framework ID:** #76

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-product-lifecycle

---

## Overview

Products go through Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline—each requiring radically different strategy and resource allocation.

---

## Four Stages

| Stage | Focus | Key Actions |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Introduction** | Launch, low growth, high investment | Awareness, trial |
| **Growth** | Market acceptance, rapid growth | Market share |
| **Maturity** | Market saturation, competition | Differentiation, retention |
| **Decline** | Decreasing demand | Cost reduction, exit |

---

## Case Study: Apple iPod (2001-2015)

| Period | Stage | Key Actions |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 2001-02 | Introduction | Premium $399, "1,000 songs" positioning |
| 2003-07 | Growth | iTunes Music Store, peak 40% of Apple revenue |
| 2008-10 | Maturity | iPhone 2007 triggered pivot; iPod repositioned niche |
| 2011-15 | Decline | Wind down major iterations |

**Peak:** 54M units (2008) → 10M (2015)

## Skill Content

```markdown
# Product Lifecycle Management

Identify a product's current stage and apply the right strategic levers for that phase.

## Steps

1. Gather signals — revenue trajectory, growth rate, competitive density, customer awareness.
2. Diagnose the stage: **Introduction** (low sales, high cost), **Growth** (rapid adoption), **Maturity** (peak revenue, slowing growth), or **Decline** (falling demand).
3. Select stage strategy — Introduction: educate and iterate; Growth: scale and differentiate; Maturity: optimize and defend; Decline: harvest, pivot, or sunset.
4. Identify transition triggers that signal the next stage is approaching.
5. Recommend specific actions, metrics to watch, and investment allocation.

## Output Format

Stage diagnosis with supporting evidence, recommended strategy, key metrics, and early-warning triggers for the next stage transition.
```
