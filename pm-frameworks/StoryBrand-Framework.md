# StoryBrand Framework (StoryBrand 故事品牌)

**Category:** Growth (增长策略)

**Framework ID:** #68

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-storybrand

---

## Overview

Uses hero's journey to restructure product communication: user is hero, product is guide, providing tools to win.

**Creator:** Donald Miller

**Year:** 2018 (Building a StoryBrand book)

---

## SB7 Framework

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Character** | Customer as protagonist; what do they want? |
| **Has a Problem** | Three-layer problem: external + inner fear + bigger meaning |
| **Meets a Guide** | You as guide with empathy + authority |
| **Gives a Plan** | Clear 3-4 step path making cooperation seem simple |
| **Calls to Action** | Dual CTAs: direct + transitional |
| **Avoids Failure** | What they lose without action |
| **End in Success** | What life looks like after action |

---

## Key Principle

Position customers as heroes, your brand as their guide (Yoda), not the hero (Luke).

---

## Case Studies

**Pantene × P&G "Strong is Beautiful":** Reframed from product-centric to customer-centric; +44% sales growth

**Basecamp Website Rewrite (2018):** Changed from "all-in-one toolkit" to "stress-free for teams that want clarity"

# StoryBrand (Donald Miller)

Position your customer as the hero and your brand as the guide using a seven-element story arc.

## Steps

1. **Character** -- Define what the customer wants (external desire)
2. **Problem** -- Identify the villain and three levels: external, internal, and philosophical
3. **Guide** -- Position your brand as the guide with empathy + authority
4. **Plan** -- Give the customer a simple 3-step plan to engage
5. **Call to Action** -- Define a clear direct CTA and a transitional CTA
6. **Success** -- Paint the picture of life after using your product
7. **Failure** -- Show what is at stake if they do not act

## Output Format

- **Hero:** [Customer] wants [desire]
- **Villain:** [Problem/antagonist]
- **Problem:** External: [X] / Internal: [X] / Philosophical: [X]
- **Guide:** We understand [empathy] and have [authority]
- **Plan:** 1. [Step] 2. [Step] 3. [Step]
- **CTA:** Direct: [X] / Transitional: [X]
- **Success:** [Transformation]
- **Failure:** [Stakes]
