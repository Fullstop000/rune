# DACI Decision Framework (DACI 决策框架)

**Category:** Execution (执行落地)

**Framework ID:** #55

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-daci

---

## Overview

For every important decision, clearly designate who drives (D), who approves (A), who contributes input (C), and who needs to be informed (I). Eliminates "no-conclusion meetings" and decision paralysis.

---

## Four Roles

| Role | Description | Count |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **D (Driver/推动者)** | Owns entire decision process, arranges meetings, collects input, ensures deadline. Does NOT make final decision. | 1 |
| **A (Approver/批准者)** | Has final decision-making authority. Listens to all input then decides. | 1 (rarely 2-3) |
| **C (Contributor/贡献者)** | Provides professional input but no voting power. Multiple people allowed. | Multiple |
| **I (Informed/知情者)** | Informed of results after decision is made. Don't participate in process. | Multiple |

---

## Key Points

1. Write DACI at top of documents/meeting invites before discussion begins
2. Never set multiple Approvers
3. Driver ≠ Approver
4. Limit Contributors
5. Always notify Informed parties after decisions
6. Don't reopen decisions once Approver decides

---

## Case Study: Intuit

**Problem:** 12 people believed they had approval authority on M&A evaluation. Decision took 4 months.

**Solution:** Reconstructed with clear DACI — D: VP Strategy, A: CPO, C: CFO, M&A Lead, Product Leads, I: Board

**Results:** Same-type decisions reduced from 4 months to 6 weeks, decision quality maintained.

description: Assign decision-making roles when a team needs to make a key product or organizational decision

# DACI Decision Framework

Clarify accountability for decisions by assigning four distinct roles: Driver, Approver, Contributors, and Informed.

## Steps

1. State the decision to be made in one clear sentence
2. Assign the **Driver** -- the single person responsible for driving the decision to completion
3. Assign the **Approver** -- the one person with veto power who makes the final call
4. List **Contributors** -- people whose input is needed before deciding
5. List **Informed** -- people who need to know the outcome but have no input
6. Set a deadline and document the decision once made

## Output Format

| Role | Person | Responsibility |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Driver | [Name] | Drives the process, gathers input, proposes recommendation |
| Approver | [Name] | Final decision authority, can veto |
| Contributors | [Names] | Provide input, expertise, or data |
| Informed | [Names] | Notified of outcome |

## Decision Record

**Decision:** [Statement]

**Deadline:** [Date]

**Outcome:** [Chosen option + rationale]
