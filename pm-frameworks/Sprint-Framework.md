# Sprint Framework (冲刺框架)

**Category:** Execution (执行落地)

**Framework ID:** #50

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-sprint

---

## Overview

Divides product development into fixed time boxes (typically 1-2 weeks), each containing Planning, Execution, Review, and Retrospective phases, enabling continuous delivery of working software.

**Framework:** Scrum

---

## Sprint Cycle

| Phase | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Sprint Planning** | Define sprint goal and tasks (half day) |
| **Execution** | Daily Scrum + Work — core phase |
| **Sprint Review** | Demo completed work (1-2 hours) |
| **Sprint Retrospective** | Reflect and improve (1 hour) |

---

## Core Roles

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Product Owner** | Manages backlog, prioritizes |
| **Scrum Master** | Facilitates process, removes blockers |
| **Development Team** | 3-9 people, cross-functional |

---

## Applicable Scenarios

- New feature iteration
- 0 to 1 product development
- Cross-functional team alignment
- Rapid market response
- Technical debt management
- Remote team synchronization

---

## Case Study: Spotify's Discover Weekly

Built in 4 two-week sprints (8 weeks total), launching to 10 million users in its first week.

## Skill Content

```markdown
# Sprint Framework

Execute work in short, time-boxed iterations with defined ceremonies to maintain focus and continuous improvement. Use for ongoing product development when you need predictable delivery cadence.

## Steps

1. **Sprint Planning** — select items from the prioritized backlog that the team commits to delivering within 1-2 weeks.
2. **Daily Standup** — each team member shares: what I did yesterday, what I will do today, any blockers (15 min max).
3. **Execution** — team works on sprint items; scope is protected from mid-sprint changes.
4. **Sprint Review** — demo completed work to stakeholders; collect feedback.
5. **Sprint Retrospective** — team reflects on what went well, what to improve, and commits to one process change.

## Output Format

Sprint artifacts: a sprint backlog with story point estimates, a burndown chart tracking daily progress, review demo notes with stakeholder feedback, and a retro summary with action items.
```
