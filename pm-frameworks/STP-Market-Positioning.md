# STP Market Positioning (STP 市场定位)

**Category:** Growth (增长策略)

**Framework ID:** #69

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-stp

---

## Overview

Three-step marketing: Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning. Addresses the "big market trap" where products try to serve everyone and end up with fuzzy positioning.

---

## Three Steps

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **S - Segmentation** | Divide market by geographic, demographic, behavioral, psychographic dimensions |
| **T - Targeting** | Evaluate segments by TAM, growth, competition, strategic fit; select 1-3 targets |
| **P - Positioning** | Establish unique value proposition in target customers' minds |

---

## Five-Step Process

1. Select segmentation dimensions (2 hours)
2. Draw segmentation market map (2 hours)
3. Evaluate and select target segments (1 hour)
4. Write positioning statement (1 hour)
5. Validate positioning-product alignment (ongoing)

---

## Case Study: Netflix (2007-2010)

Repositioned from DVD rental to streaming platform, targeting heavy series consumers and using personalization algorithms as core differentiation.

# STP Marketing Strategy

Divide the market into segments, choose the most attractive ones, and craft a positioning for each.

## Steps

1. **Segment** the market using demographic, behavioral, psychographic, or needs-based criteria
2. Profile each segment with size, growth rate, needs, and willingness to pay
3. **Target** -- evaluate segments on attractiveness (size, growth, competition) and fit (capabilities, mission)
4. Select primary and secondary target segments
5. **Position** -- craft a positioning statement for each target: "For [segment] who [need], [product] is [category] that [differentiator] because [reason to believe]"
