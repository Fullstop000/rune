# First Principles (第一性原理)

**Category:** Systems Thinking (系统思维)

## Overview

A thinking approach that rejects analogical reasoning and experientialism. Decompose problems to their most fundamental, indivisible facts, then rebuild solutions from those facts. Elon Musk used this method to reduce rocket manufacturing costs by 90%.

**Creator:** Elon Musk (modern popularization)

**Type:** Cognitive Framework

**Year:** Modern popularization ~2013

---

## What It Solves

Most thinking is analogical—observing others and making incremental improvements. First principles asks: "What do we know with absolute certainty from physics and logic?" This is harder and slower, but the only path to 10x improvement rather than 10%.

| Thinking Type | Approach | Result |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| **Analogical** | "Industry best practice is X, we follow" | 10% improvement |
| **First Principles** | "What is physically necessary vs. convention?" | 10x improvement |

---

## Three-Step Framework

**Step 1: Identify Current Assumptions**
List all taken-for-granted assumptions. This is hardest—deepest assumptions are often ones you don't realize you're making.

**Step 2: Strip to Basic Facts**
Persistently ask "Why? What is it actually made of?" until reaching physically/logically indivisible layers.

**Step 3: Rebuild from Facts**
Redesign solutions based on what you now know to be true, unconstrained by original industry conventions.

---

## Core Questions by Domain

- **Product Design:** "What task do users actually need to complete?"
- **Pricing:** "If starting from zero, without knowing competitors, what should pricing be?"
- **Organization:** "If redesigned today, what is the optimal structure?"
- **Technology:** "What capabilities do we truly need?" (not "what's popular")
- **Cost:** "How much is physics vs. supply chain inertia?"

---

## Case Study: SpaceX Rocket Cost Revolution

**The Challenge (2002):** Russian quote for rockets: $8M each. Industry consensus: $65M per launch.

**First Principles Breakdown:**
- Rocket = aluminum, titanium, copper, carbon fiber
- Raw material market price ≈ 3% of finished rocket cost
- Conclusion: 97% is assumption, not physics

**Rebuild Result:**
- SpaceX builds all components in-house
- Falcon 1 launch cost: $7M (vs. industry $65M)
- Falcon 9 booster recovery: reduces cost another 50%

**Outcome (2023):** SpaceX launches more than all other nations combined; Starlink achieves global coverage with 4,000+ satellites.

---

## Usage Tips

1. Start with "Must it really be this way?"—not "How can we do it better?"
2. Distinguish physical/logical truth from industry convention
3. Results often violate common sense—meaningful when counterintuitive
4. Don't use for mature, low-uncertainty problems; use analogical thinking there
5. Validate with numbers: "Raw material cost is X%, physical limit is Y"

# First Principles Thinking

Strip away assumptions and conventions to reason from foundational truths, then build up novel solutions.

## Steps

1. **State the problem** clearly and identify the current conventional approach
2. **List assumptions** -- write down every assumption embedded in the current approach
3. **Challenge each assumption** -- ask "Is this actually true? Why?" for each one
4. **Identify fundamental truths** -- what remains after stripping away assumptions?
5. **Rebuild from the ground up** -- construct new solutions using only the fundamentals
6. Compare the first-principles solution to the conventional one
