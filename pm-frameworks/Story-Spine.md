# Story Spine (故事骨架法)

**Category:** Ideation (创意生成)

**Framework ID:** #30

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-story-spine

---

## Overview

Uses Pixar's classic narrative template to construct product vision stories, aligning team and stakeholders on value proposition.

**Creator:** Kenn Adams (1991, improv comedy)

**Popularized by:** Pixar

---

## The Six Sentences

| Step | Starter | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| 01 | "Once upon a time…" | Establish user background & context |
| 02 | "Every day…" | Describe current state/normal behavior |
| 03 | "Until one day…" | The trigger event that disrupts status quo |
| 04 | "Because of that…" | Consequential results (repeatable) |
| 05 | "Until finally…" | Core transformation/climax |
| 06 | "Ever since then…" | New normal/product promise |

---

## Key Steps

1. **Select scenario (15 min)** — Choose a user scenario showing strongest "before vs. after" contrast
2. **Fill first two sentences (20 min)** — Describe user identity specifically, not generically
3. **Find the turning point (30 min)** — External trigger—not product discovery, but disruption
4. **Connect causal chain (20 min)** — 2-4 "Because of that" repetitions show design decision points
5. **Validate "ever since then" (15 min)** — Test against real user feedback or product hypotheses

---

## Applicable Scenarios

- Product Pitch preparation
- PRD user scenario descriptions
- User interview data synthesis
- Onboarding flow narrative design
- Investor roadshow storytelling
- Team alignment on user problem understanding

---

## Case Study: Finding Nemo (2003) by Pixar/Andrew Stanton

- Spine established film's emotional skeleton
- 6-sentence arc: overprotective father → restrictions → son captured → journey begins → fear overcome → new relationship with ocean
- **Result:** $867M global box office

# Story Spine (Kenn Adams / Pixar)

Build a narrative arc for your product vision or user journey using a simple fill-in-the-blank story structure. Use when you need to communicate product vision, write a pitch, or frame a user problem compellingly.

## Steps

1. **"Once upon a time..."** — Describe the world/user before your product.
2. **"Every day..."** — Describe the routine or status quo.
3. **"Until one day..."** — Introduce the disruption or problem.
4. **"Because of that..."** (repeat 2-3x) — Show cascading consequences.
5. **"Until finally..."** — Reveal how your product resolves the tension.
6. **"And ever since that day..."** — Describe the new, better reality.

## Output Format

A short narrative (6-10 sentences) following the Story Spine template, suitable for a pitch deck slide, PRD intro, or stakeholder presentation.
