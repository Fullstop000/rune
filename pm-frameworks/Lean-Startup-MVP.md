# Lean Startup MVP (精益创业 MVP)

**Category:** Validation (验证测试)

**Framework ID:** #32

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-lean-startup-mvp

---

## Overview

A methodology for validating the most critical business hypotheses using a Minimum Viable Product. The approach emphasizes the "Build→Measure→Learn" rapid feedback loop to find the right direction through real user feedback, avoiding large-scale ineffective investments.

**Creator:** Eric Ries

**Year:** 2008-2011

---

## What It Solves

The framework addresses teams building complete products before validation. Traditional product development assumes teams know what users want. This leads to months or years of development only to discover users don't care—or want something entirely different. The fatal flaw is receiving feedback too late.

---

## Build-Measure-Learn Loop

The core is a cycle, not a product. The goal is to spin this loop as fast as possible, generating real learning with each iteration:

- **Build** - Construct the minimum version to test
- **Measure** - Collect data on user response
- **Learn** - Validate or invalidate the hypothesis

---

## Five MVP Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **视频 MVP** | Demonstrate concept with a demo video before the product exists | Dropbox |
| **落地页 MVP** | Measure real demand through a login page | Email waitlist |
| **礼宾 MVP** | Manually serve a small number of users first | Early Airbnb |
| **巫师奥兹 MVP** | Users see "automated product" but operations are manual | Zappos |
| **功能最小化 MVP** | Only one core feature; nothing else | Single-feature launch |

---

## Case Study: Dropbox

**The Story:** In 2007, Drew Houston created a 3-minute demo video showing Dropbox's functionality before writing a single line of cloud sync code.

**The MVP Cost:** Approximately $0, 1 day of work

**The Process:**
- **Build:** Created a 3-minute screen recording demonstrating how Dropbox would work, posted to Hacker News
- **Measure:** Beta waitlist grew from 5,000 to 75,000 registrations overnight
- **Learn:** Demand was validated. No feature specifications or pitch deck—just confirmation of product direction

**Result:** Dropbox ultimately reached 500 million users.

---

## Five Principles

1. **MVP is about hypothesis validation, not feature minimization** - Before designing an MVP, write down your biggest assumption.
2. **List and prioritize dangerous assumptions** - Test the most important ones first; teams often avoid truly dangerous hypotheses.
3. **"Perfect" is the enemy of MVP** - Eric Ries: "If you're not embarrassed by your first version, you launched too late."
4. **Define metrics before building** - Establish what data would validate or invalidate the hypothesis before releasing the MVP.
5. **Pivot is a normal outcome, not failure** - The two possible results are persistence or pivot. Pivoting is learning something closer to the truth.

# Lean Startup MVP

This methodology guides you through validating product ideas using the Build-Measure-Learn loop with minimal investment.

**Core Steps:**
1. Identify your riskiest assumption
2. Define measurable success metrics
3. Build the smallest possible test (MVP)
4. Measure real user behavior
5. Decide: persevere, pivot, or kill

**Output:** A one-page MVP spec including the core hypothesis, clear success/failure thresholds, scope definition, measurement plan, and a decision framework for next steps.
