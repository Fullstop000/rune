# SWOT Analysis (SWOT 分析)

**Category:** Definition (问题定义)

**Framework ID:** #17

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-swot

---

## Overview

SWOT analyzes Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats - four dimensions to systematically examine product or enterprise status, establishing shared language for strategic decisions.

**Creator:** Albert Humphrey (Stanford Research Institute)

**Year:** 1960s-1970s

---

## The Four Quadrants

| Quadrant | Type | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **S (Strengths)** | Internal·Positive | Brand loyalty, ecosystem, industrial design |
| **W (Weaknesses)** | Internal·Negative | No phone hardware experience, carrier dependency |
| **O (Opportunities)** | External·Positive | Mobile internet growth, poor existing smartphone UX |
| **T (Threats)** | External·Negative | Nokia 40% market share, carrier control |

---

## Strategy Matrix

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **SO** | Use strengths to seize opportunities |
| **WO** | Overcome weaknesses with opportunities |
| **ST** | Use strengths to counter threats |
| **WT** | Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats |

---

## Usage Tips

1. Only list 3-5 most important factors per quadrant
2. Update at every major strategic inflection point
3. Be honest about weaknesses
4. Distinguish internal vs. external factors
5. Team of 2+ people
6. Must create SO/WO/ST/WT strategy matrix

---

## Case Study: Apple 2007 iPhone Launch

- **Counterintuitive decision:** AT&T exclusivity (W) in exchange for iOS software full control (ST strategy)
- **Deliberate weakness:** v1 no third-party App support was active WT strategy to focus on core experience

## Skill Download

**File:** `skills/swot.md`

**Skill Content:**

```markdown
# SWOT Analysis

Evaluate internal capabilities and external conditions to inform strategic decisions.

## Steps

1. **Strengths** — list internal advantages: unique resources, capabilities, brand, team, technology.
2. **Weaknesses** — list internal limitations: skill gaps, resource constraints, process inefficiencies.
3. **Opportunities** — identify external favorable conditions: market trends, unmet needs, regulatory changes.
4. **Threats** — identify external risks: competitors, market shifts, economic downturns, technology disruption.
5. Cross-reference quadrants to generate strategies: SO (leverage), WO (improve), ST (defend), WT (avoid).

## Output Format

2x2 SWOT matrix with bullet points per quadrant, plus a TOWS strategy table with specific actions for each SO/WO/ST/WT combination.
```
