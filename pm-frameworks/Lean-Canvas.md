## Overview

One-page strategic tool created by Ash Maurya in 2010, adapted from Business Model Canvas for early-stage startups. Replaces BMC modules less relevant to early teams with validation-focused blocks.

**Creator:** Ash Maurya

**Year:** 2010

---

## Nine Blocks

| Block | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Problem** | Top 3 customer pain points and existing alternatives |
| **Solution** | Simplest viable solution for each problem |
| **Unique Value Proposition (UVP)** | One-sentence summary of what you are, for whom, and why you're different |
| **Unfair Advantage** | Something competitors cannot easily copy or buy |
| **Customer Segments** | Target customers and early adopter traits |
| **Channels** | How to reach customers |
| **Key Metrics** | One north star metric only |
| **Cost Structure** | Major costs and break-even needs |
| **Revenue Streams** | What customers pay for and pricing logic |

---

## Use Cases

- Assumption mapping before coding
- Co-founder alignment
- Investor pitch preparation
- Post-pivot strategy redefinition
- Corporate innovation projects
- Competitive analysis and differentiation

---

## Case Study: Buffer

Joel Gascoigne built Buffer using Lean Canvas methodology. Before writing code, he created a landing page with only two buttons ("Pricing" and "Plans"). **120 people registered in the first week**, validating demand before product construction.

---

## Five Usage Principles

1. One page is a constraint forcing focus
2. Fill UVP first as the anchor point
3. Leaving "Unfair Advantage" blank is honest
4. Update after every founder/investor conversation
5. Only one north star metric—not a list

## Skill Download

**File:** `skills/lean-canvas.md`

**Skill Content:**

```markdown
# Lean Canvas (Ash Maurya)

Capture your entire business model on a single page by filling in nine blocks. Use when starting a new product or startup and you need to quickly articulate and stress-test the business model.

## Steps

1. **Problem** — list the top 3 problems your target customers face.
2. **Customer Segments** — define who has these problems; identify early adopters.
3. **Unique Value Proposition** — write one clear sentence on why you are different and worth attention.
4. **Solution** — describe the simplest solution for each problem.
5. **Channels** — list how you will reach your customers.
6. **Revenue Streams** — define how you will make money.
7. **Cost Structure** — list major costs.
8. **Key Metrics** — identify 3-5 numbers that tell you the business is working.
9. **Unfair Advantage** — state what cannot be easily copied or bought.

## Output Format

A single-page canvas grid with all nine blocks filled in concisely — each block should be 2-4 bullet points maximum.
```
