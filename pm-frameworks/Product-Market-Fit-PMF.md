## Overview

PMF is prerequisite for growth. Uses "40% test" and retention curves to determine if PMF has been achieved.

**Creator:** Marc Andreesen

---

## Measuring PMF

### Sean Ellis Test (40% Rule)

Ask users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"
- If >40% say "Very disappointed" = PMF achieved

### Retention Curves

- Good retention curves plateau at meaningful level
- Poor retention curves continue declining

---

## Path to PMF

1. **Measure** — Track key metrics
2. **Iterate** — Improve based on feedback
3. **Validate** — Confirm PMF achieved
4. **Scale** — Invest in growth

# Product-Market Fit (PMF)

Measure whether your product satisfies strong market demand. Use when you need to assess readiness for growth or diagnose stall.

## Steps

1. **Run the Sean Ellis test** -- ask users "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?" Target >40% "Very disappointed."
2. **Analyze retention curves** -- plot retention over time cohorts. Healthy products plateau; unhealthy products decline.
3. **Identify leading indicators** -- look for organic growth, high NPS, strong engagement patterns.
4. **If PMF not achieved** -- focus on improving product until PMF metrics are hit.
5. **If PMF achieved** -- begin scaling investment while monitoring for PMF erosion.

## Output Format

**PMF Score:** [Percentage of users "Very disappointed"]

**Retention Analysis:** [Cohort curves by signup month]

**PMF Status:** [Achieved / Not Achieved / At Risk]

**Key Actions:** [Next steps based on PMF status]
