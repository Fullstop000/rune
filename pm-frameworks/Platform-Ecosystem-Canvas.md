**Category:** Systems Thinking (系统思维)

**Framework ID:** #75

## Overview

Designing multi-sided platforms requires defining participants, value exchange rules, entry thresholds, and governance mechanisms.

---

## Key Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Participants** | Who uses the platform |
| **Value exchange** | How value flows between participants |
| **Entry thresholds** | What barriers exist for participants |
| **Governance** | Rules and enforcement mechanisms |
| **Core interaction** | The core value creation mechanism |

---

## Case Study: WeChat (2011-2017)

- Evolution from messaging tool to super-platform ecosystem
- Key decision: Mini Programs reduced developer friction ~80%
- Result: 500万+ mini programs, 万亿+ RMB annual transactions

# Platform Ecosystem Canvas

Design and analyze platform businesses by mapping the core interaction and the ecosystem that enables it.

## Steps

1. Define the **Core Interaction** -- what is the single most important exchange on the platform?
2. Identify **Participants** -- who are the producers and consumers? Are there multiple sides?
3. Define the **Value Unit** -- what is created and exchanged (listing, post, video, data)?
4. Design **Filters** -- how does the platform curate and match supply to demand (search, algorithm, curation)?
5. Map **governance rules** -- what rules keep quality high and prevent abuse?
6. Identify the **chicken-and-egg** problem and the strategy to solve it

## Output Format

- **Core Interaction:** [Producer] creates [Value Unit] for [Consumer]
- **Producers:** [Who and what they contribute]
- **Consumers:** [Who and what they seek]
- **Value Unit:** [What is exchanged]
- **Filters:** [Matching/curation mechanism]
- **Governance:** [Quality and trust rules]
- **Cold start strategy:** [How to solve chicken-and-egg]
