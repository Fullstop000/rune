# Six Thinking Hats (六顶思考帽)

**Category:** Ideation (创意生成)

**Framework ID:** #24

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-six-thinking-hats

---

## Overview

A framework using six colored hats to represent six distinct thinking modes, enabling teams to focus on the same thinking approach simultaneously and eliminate role-based conflicts in meetings.

**Creator:** Edward de Bono

**Year:** 1985

---

## The Six Hats

| Hat | Name | Focus |
|-----|------|-------|
| **White** | 白帽—事实 | Facts and data only |
| **Red** | 红帽—情绪 | Emotions and intuition |
| **Black** | 黑帽—风险 | Critical judgment, risks |
| **Yellow** | 黄帽—价值 | Optimism, benefits |
| **Green** | 绿帽—创意 | Creative possibilities |
| **Blue** | 蓝帽—流程 | Process management |

---

## Key Steps (90-Min Meeting Agenda)

| Time | Hat | Focus |
|------|-----|-------|
| 0–5 min | Blue Hat | Clarify objective and hat sequence |
| 5–20 min | White Hat | List all known facts and data |
| 20–35 min | Yellow Hat | Explore benefits only (no "buts") |
| 35–55 min | Black Hat | Identify risks and logical flaws |
| 55–70 min | Green Hat | Generate alternative solutions |
| 70–80 min | Red Hat | Share gut feelings in one sentence |
| 80–90 min | Blue Hat | Form conclusions and next steps |

---

## Case Study: NASA × Challenger Disaster (1986)

The Challenger shuttle explosion revealed a critical organizational flaw: engineers' "Black Hat" risk warnings about O-ring failures in cold temperatures were suppressed by management's "Yellow Hat" optimism and political pressures. The framework demonstrates that Black Hat thinking requires a legitimately safe space for expression.

## Skill Download

**File:** `skills/six-thinking-hats.md`

**Skill Content:**

```markdown
# Six Thinking Hats (de Bono)

Evaluate a topic by systematically switching between six distinct thinking modes, ensuring balanced analysis and preventing groupthink.

## Steps

1. **White Hat (Facts)** — What data and information do we have? What is missing? Focus only on known facts and gaps.
2. **Red Hat (Feelings)** — What is your gut reaction? What emotions does this idea trigger? No justification needed.
3. **Black Hat (Caution)** — What could go wrong? What are the risks, weaknesses, and obstacles?
4. **Yellow Hat (Optimism)** — What are the benefits? Why could this work? What is the best-case scenario?
5. **Green Hat (Creativity)** — What alternatives exist? What new ideas does this spark? Think laterally.
6. **Blue Hat (Process)** — Step back and manage the thinking. Summarize findings, decide next steps, and identify which hat needs more time.

## Output Format

A six-section analysis, one per hat color, with key points listed under each. Conclude with a Blue Hat summary that synthesizes all perspectives and recommends a decision or next action.
```
