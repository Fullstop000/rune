# Problem-Solution Fit (问题-方案契合)

**Category:** Validation (验证测试)

**Framework ID:** #40

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-problem-solution-fit

---

## Overview

A validation framework that precedes Product-Market Fit. Answers two questions: (1) Do your target customers actually have the problem you assume? (2) Can your solution actually solve it?

---

## Three-Stage Validation Progression

| Stage | Method | Pass Criteria |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Stage 01: Problem Interviews | Qualitative interviews × 20 | 60%+ describe same pain point without prompting, rated as "very painful" |
| Stage 02: Solution Interviews | Prototype/sketch demonstration × 10 | 70%+ say they would use it; 30%+ willing to pay |
| Stage 03: Early User Commitment | Real commitments (pre-payment, LOI, referrals) | 5+ real commitments = PSF achieved |

---

## Case Study: Superhuman (2014-2017)

| Phase | Key Actions |
|-------|-------------|
| Problem Interviews | 100+ deep interviews with "high-frequency email users" using only one question: "Walk me through your email workflow and tell me where you feel pain" |
| Solution Interviews | Concept: keyboard shortcuts, <100ms load time. Users responded: "I would pay $30/month for this" |
| Commitment Validation | 5,000 users pre-paid $30/month for 6-month waitlist with no product to test |

**Metrics:**
- 100+ problem interviews (no solution mentions)
- 5,000 pre-paid waitlist users (pre-launch)
- $33M Series B (2019)

## Skill Download

**File:** `skills/problem-solution-fit.md`

**Skill Content:**

```markdown
# Problem-Solution Fit

Confirm that the problem is real, that users care about it, and that your solution addresses it effectively. Use before investing in full product development to avoid building something nobody wants.

## Steps

1. **Problem validation** — interview 10+ target users to confirm the problem exists, is frequent, and is painful.
2. **Current alternatives** — document how users solve the problem today and where those solutions fall short.
3. **Solution demo** — present your proposed solution (prototype, mockup, or description) to the same users.
4. **Fit assessment** — measure user reactions: willingness to switch, willingness to pay, excitement level.
5. **Iterate or proceed** — if fit is weak, refine the solution and retest; if fit is strong, move to MVP.

## Output Format

A fit report with: problem evidence (user quotes, frequency data), current alternatives analysis, solution feedback summary, a fit score (strong/weak/no fit), and recommended next steps.
```
