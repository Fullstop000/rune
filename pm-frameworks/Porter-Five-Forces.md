# Porter Five Forces (波特五力模型)

**Category:** Systems Thinking (系统思维)

**Framework ID:** #82

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-porters-five-forces

---

## Overview

Analyzes industry structure across five dimensions: supplier power, buyer power, new entrant threat, substitute threat, competitive intensity.

**Creator:** Michael Porter (Harvard Business School)

**Year:** 1979

---

## Five Forces

| Force | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Supplier Power** | How much power do suppliers have? |
| **Buyer Power** | How much power do customers have? |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | How intense is competition? |
| **Threat of New Entrants** | How easy is it to enter? |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | How easily can alternatives replace you? |

---

## Industry Attractiveness

Strong forces = unattractive industry / Weak forces = attractive industry

# Porter's Five Forces

Analyze the structural forces that shape competition and profitability in an industry.

## Steps

1. **Supplier Power** — assess supplier concentration, switching costs, and substitute inputs.
2. **Buyer Power** — evaluate buyer concentration, price sensitivity, and switching costs.
3. **Threat of New Entrants** — examine barriers to entry (capital, regulation, network effects, brand).
4. **Threat of Substitutes** — identify alternative solutions and their price-performance tradeoff.
5. **Competitive Rivalry** — assess number of competitors, differentiation, exit barriers, and growth rate.

## Output Format

Five-forces summary table rating each force (High/Medium/Low) with supporting evidence, overall industry attractiveness assessment, and strategic implications.
