# Crazy 8s (Crazy 8s)

**Category:** Ideation (创意生成)

**Framework ID:** #26

## Overview

Fold paper into 8 panels, set 8-minute timer, draw a different idea in each panel. Time pressure bypasses the inner critic and forces diversity beyond obvious answers.

**Creator:** Jake Knapp, Google Ventures

**Year:** 2010 (incorporated into GV Design Sprint 2012)

---

## What It Solves

Creative meetings fail because: loudest person dominates, self-censorship kills ideas before they're spoken, and teams default to safe answers. Crazy 8s uses physical constraints (time + space limits) to bypass social filtering and mental critics. The key insight: ideas 6-8 are where real creativity emerges—after obvious solutions are exhausted.

---

## Core Mechanics

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Format** | Paper folded into 8 equal panels |
| **Time** | 8 minutes total (60 seconds per panel) |
| **Output** | 8 genuinely different directions |
| **Participation** | Silent, individual, simultaneous |
| **Rule** | Never stop drawing to complete a panel |

---

## Key Benefits

1. Time pressure eliminates self-judgment
2. Hand-drawing is 10x faster than digital tools
3. 8 panels force you beyond obvious answers
4. Equal participation regardless of seniority

---

## Famous Case Studies

**GV × Slack Sprint (2014):** During Slack's pre-launch design sprint, a participant sketched "a bot with personality that greets users in a new workspace"—rough, nameless, in panel 7. This became Slackbot, their signature onboarding assistant and retention mechanism.

**Airbnb 2012:** A "large photo" sketch won their sprint—this became the visual foundation of Airbnb's "belong anywhere" brand identity for the next decade.

---

## Implementation Rules

1. Paper and pen only—digital tools slow you down
2. Strict 60 seconds per panel, no extensions
3. Each panel must be a different direction, not variations
4. Complete silence until timer ends
5. Prefer incomplete sketches—they capture raw concepts
6. Follow with structured share + dot voting

# Crazy 8s

A rapid ideation exercise where participants sketch 8 distinct ideas in 8 minutes, forcing fast divergent thinking and preventing over-attachment to a single concept.

## Steps

1. **Set the challenge** — Define the specific problem or "How Might We" question to solve.
2. **Prepare the sheet** — Fold a sheet of paper into 8 panels (or use 8 digital frames).
3. **Sketch 8 ideas in 8 minutes** — One idea per panel, one minute each. Prioritize quantity and variety over polish.
4. **Present and discuss** — Each participant shares their 8 sketches. Others note ideas they find compelling (no critique yet).
5. **Select and refine** — Vote on the strongest concepts, then develop the top 1-2 into more detailed solution sketches.

## Output Format

8 quick concept sketches (visual or described in bullet points), each with a short title. Followed by vote tallies and a refined description of the top-voted concept(s).
