# SCAMPER

**Category:** Ideation (创意生成)

**Framework ID:** #23

**Source:** https://pmframe.works/framework-scamper

---

## Overview

Seven systematic creative strategies—Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse—designed to ensure innovative thinking doesn't get stuck in conventional paths.

**Creator:** Bob Eberle (1971), based on Alex Osborn's 1950s creative checklist

---

## The Seven Strategies

| Letter | Strategy | Key Question |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **S** | Substitute | What if you replace materials, people, or processes with something else? |
| **C** | Combine | What happens when you merge two or more ideas, functions, or products? |
| **A** | Adapt | How can solutions from other industries or fields be borrowed and modified? |
| **M** | Modify | What if you change the form, appearance, size, speed, or perception? |
| **P** | Put to other uses | How else could this existing product or function be used? |
| **E** | Eliminate | What happens if you remove a part, step, or element entirely? |
| **R** | Reverse | What if you flip the order, direction, roles, or assumptions? |

---

## Key Steps

1. Start with a specific product or process as your reference object
2. Apply each SCAMPER perspective, spending at least 5 minutes per angle
3. Generate 3–5 ideas per perspective
4. When stuck on one angle, move to the next
5. Combine multiple perspectives for the most disruptive outcomes

---

## When to Use

- Product redesign workshops
- Feature innovation sprints
- Competitive response planning
- Startup business pivots
- Internal process optimization
- Hackathon creative guidance

---

## Case Study: Apple iPod (2001)

- **S** → Hard drive replaced flash memory → "1,000 songs in your pocket"
- **C** → Combined player + iTunes + iTunes Store into one ecosystem
- **A** → Adapted the click wheel interaction from mechanical dials
- **M** → Made it 60% thinner than competitors
- **P** → Repositioned as portable storage for business users
- **E** → Eliminated physical media entirely
- **R** → Flipped from "you go to the record store" to "music comes to you"

**Result:** 100 million units sold in six years, transformed the entire music industry.

## Skill Download

**File:** `skills/scamper.md`

**Skill Content:**

```markdown
# SCAMPER

A structured brainstorming technique that applies seven transformation lenses to an existing product or idea to generate creative alternatives.

## Steps

1. **Substitute** — What components, materials, or people could you swap out? What alternative would work?
2. **Combine** — What ideas, features, or functions could you merge? What would a hybrid look like?
3. **Adapt** — What could you borrow from another domain or context? What existing solution could be adapted?
4. **Modify** — What could you enlarge, shrink, or change in shape, color, or form? What if you exaggerated one aspect?
5. **Put to other use** — Could this product serve a different user or market? What other problems could it solve?
6. **Eliminate** — What could you remove or simplify? What is non-essential?
7. **Reverse / Rearrange** — What if you reversed the sequence, flipped the model, or rearranged the layout?

## Output Format

A table with one row per SCAMPER letter. Columns: Lens, Prompt Question, Generated Ideas (2-3 per lens), and a final shortlist of the most promising ideas with rationale.
```
